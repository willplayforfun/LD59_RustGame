NEW IDEA:

Stars. See spectrum and brightness and position. Adjust for red shift. Identify periodic wobbles, dimming, red/blue shift to identify planets.

Flow:

Game starts. Black screen fade into starfield. 
Text types out: Survey 1
A box starts screen edge and narrows in to identify one star.
Text fades away, and a window opens containing an image of the star on the left, and three graphs in the middle: brightness over time, red shift over time, and relative position over time.
To the right is a UI where the player can add planets and adjust their mass, orbit eccentricity, and orbit period with... sliders? and there are also buttons to move in big and small increments.
A second line on each graph, in red rather than black, shows the predicted values given the planets that are plugged in.
If the player presses a button at the bottom of the screen, any planet that is close enough to the real values of one of the planets is accepted with a green check. Invalid ones are crossed out with a red X.
